Meet the Crew

Tony Howard - Owner and Director of Operations

Read more...

Anthony Howard is the owner and Director of Operations at Danzversity and a lifelong student of entrepreneurship and the arts. He believes in the power of dance to positively influence people and create a culture of freedom. His desire is for every student to feel empowered, encouraged, and equipped to confidently express themselves through the art of dance. Anthony received his Bachelor of Science Degree in Mechanical Engineering from The University of Texas at San Antonio. He has held professional positions include being a mechanical design engineer, project engineer and co-director of a non-profit.

Jaymie Orozco Howard - Owner and Dance Facilitator

Read more...

Jaymie Howard is the owner of Danzversity and a true educational visionary. She has taught in both public and private schools for the last 5 years and has been a committed dancer for the past 15 years. Jaymie’s desire is to create a safe, welcoming space where diversity flourishes, freedom abounds, and every student is positively impacted. Her energetic and genuine teaching spirit are what make Danzversity so unique. Jaymie earned her degree from Texas State University with a major in Interdisciplinary Studies, and she is committed to empowering the community through social service.

St.Matthew - Fitness Guru, Coach and Dance Facilitator

Read more...

St. Matthew Ellis is the lead fitness and dance instructor of Danzversity. Heavily influenced by Carribbean, Latin, African, and American Hip-Hop culture, Matthew’s dancing is a rich mixture of heritiage and never-before-seen innovation. Born in Port Antonio, Jamaica, Matthew grew up with a love for soccer and dance. At the age of 11, Matthew started Sugar Crew, a dance group that performed for five years. After moving to Fullerton, California, at the age of 16, Matthew formed a five member dance group called Gospel Dancers. A year later, Matthew found himself in St. Petersburg, Russia, forming an eight-person dance group called Worship Dancers. Back in the States, Matthew earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Economics and a Master of Arts degree in Leadership Studies and Public Administration from the University of Texas at El Paso. Passionate about the community, Matthew would one day like to coach high school football.

Hassan Christopher - Master Dance Facilitator

Read more...

Hassan Christopher is an award winning director/choreographer and master instructor. He is honored to have earned the Lester Horton Innovator Award, an NAACP award for Best Stage Choreography, a New York Musical Festival Award for Excellence, and numerous nominations. His work as a performer and director/choreographer has been seen at venues nationally and abroad including the Guggenheim, REDCAT, Socrates Sculpture Park, UCLA, Grand Performances among others. In addition to self-producing new and original projects, his freelance credits include choreography for Comedy Central's Chocolate News and the Naked Trucker & T-Bone show and music videos for TVOTR, Shakira, Usher and Britney Spears. Hassan is also a passionate movement educator with over a decade of teaching experience in contemporary, jazz, partnering, improvisation, choreography and urban dance forms. He has taught at leading studios including the Edge in Hollywood and served as a guest lecturer and teaching artist for UCLA, LMU, Scripps College and the American Musical and Dramatic Academy in Los Angeles and New York. Most recently he did movement direction for rising pop sensation and singer/trumpet player Spencer Ludwig and co-created a full-length site-specific work that earned Critic's Pick in Time Out New York.

Ursula Cimone Walker - Theater, Song and Dance Facilitator

Read more...

Ursula Cimone is a creative artista with a strong background in the performative arts. Her love for diversity in the arts has fueled her engagement in various expressive mediums since childhood. Though movement is one of her favorite forms, her true passion is the product of many experiences. After 5 years of dancing with Ordered Steps Productions, a unique ministry of dance based in Dallas, she realized the powerful spiritual element of dance. While studying Theatre and Dance at UT Austin, she was in a minor car accident that led to a temporary inability to dance. Recovering from this and the resulting depression, she found a passion for movement and sound as modes of healing. Her experiences with an Austin-based ensemble, the Afro Caribbean Soul Collective, inspired a profound openness of artistic flow that she wished to share with youth in the community. This past year she found herself teaching with AISD, which opened her eyes to the effect of emotional trauma in educational spaces. Further, it showed her how transformative art can be for students who suffer those traumas. These experiences have all culminated into a passion for creating safe spaces where art can be a catalyst for emotional release and spiritual wellbeing. Collaborating at Danzversity is a step towards this vision, and she is humbled and thrilled to be apart of the journey! (She also gives a shoutout to her mom for the the creative opportunities she provided our Dallas community with, and for inspiring Ursula to keep it going :)

Kayla McLeod - Dance Facilitator and Outreach Events Coordinator

Read more...

Kayla is a Nashville-native enjoying the teachings of life. With a Jamaican father and a Guyanese mother, Kayla grew up with movement imbedded into the fiber of her being. Dancing from middle school on, ballet, jazz, contemporary, hip hop, lyrical, pole dance, and modern have all influenced her style. She attended Ursinus College in Collegeville, Pennsylvania, where she majored in both Biology and Dance. When not dancing, she’s learning self-love and self-care with yoga, Octavia Butler, Marvel Films & Comics, and Tumblr all mixed in between. Kayla also enjoys singing, horseback riding (English), and learning how to cook one dish at a time. And Dogs. Always Dogs.
